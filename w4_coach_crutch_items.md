# Coach vs. Crutch Strategy Items

Scale: Never / Rarely / Sometimes / Often / Always

Intro: "People use AI in many different ways. There are no right or wrong answers — we want to know what **you** actually do."

---

## Current items (3 coach + 3 crutch)

| # | Construct | Item |
|---|-----------|------|
| 23 | **Coach** — try-first | Before asking AI for help, I try to **work through the task on my own first**. |
| 24 | **Coach** — Socratic | I ask AI **follow-up questions** to make sure I really understand what it told me. |
| 25 | **Coach** — evaluation | Before I use what AI gives me, I **check whether it's correct**. |
| 26 | **Crutch** — passive consumption | I tend to **use what AI gives me without making changes**. |
| 27 | **Crutch** — difficulty avoidance | I use AI to **avoid tasks I find difficult or challenging**. |
| 28 | **Crutch** — initiation dependency | I find it **hard to get started** on tasks without asking AI first. |

---

## Dropped items

| Item | Reason dropped |
|------|---------------|
| ~~I use AI to learn things I'd have trouble learning on my own~~ | Learning orientation — motivation not strategy, overlaps with outcome agency |
| ~~I keep asking AI to revise what it gives me until I get what I want~~ | Iterative refinement — ambiguous coach/crutch |
| ~~I ask AI for feedback on my ideas or writing the way I'd ask a coach or teacher~~ | Input-seeking — social desirability ceiling, overlaps with Socratic item |

## Design notes

- **Previous version** had 6 coach + 1 crutch. Pilot showed coach and crutch were orthogonal (r = .00), but measurement was lopsided.
- **Rebalance**: Cut 3 weakest coach items, added 2 new crutch items (difficulty avoidance, initiation dependency).
- Items are randomized within the block (all 6 together).
- Frequency scale chosen over agree/disagree because items are behavioral.
